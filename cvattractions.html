Local Attractions

Museo Cappella Sansevero

Located in the heart of Naples' historic center, the Museo Cappella Sansevero is renowned for housing the "Veiled Christ," one of the most remarkable sculptures worldwide. The chapel intertwines Baroque creativity, dynastic pride, beauty, and mystery, reflecting the ingenuity of its creator, Prince Raimondo di Sangro.

Galleria Borbonica

The Galleria Borbonica represents a fascinating journey into Naples' underground. Constructed in the 19th century as an escape route for the royal family, today it offers visitors the chance to explore tunnels, cisterns, and World War II air-raid shelters, silent witnesses to the city's history.

Napoli Sotterranea

A journey 40 meters deep to discover another side of Naples. Among tunnels, cisterns, and archaeological remains, Napoli Sotterranea reveals the city's millennia-old history, from its Greek origins to World War II.

Naples Cathedral and Treasure of San Gennaro

The Naples Cathedral, also known as the Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta, is the city's main place of worship. It houses the Treasure of San Gennaro, the city's patron saint, famous for the miracle of the liquefaction of his blood, attracting devotees from around the world.

Castel dell'Ovo

Located on the islet of Megaride, Castel dell'Ovo is the oldest castle in Naples. It offers a panoramic view of the Gulf and encloses fascinating legends related to the city's origins.

Piazza del Plebiscito

The pulsating heart of Naples, Piazza del Plebiscito is one of the largest squares in Italy. Surrounded by historic buildings such as the Royal Palace and the Basilica of San Francesco di Paola, it is a venue for events, concerts, and cultural manifestations.

Maschio Angioino

Maschio Angioino, also known as Castel Nuovo, is a medieval castle that has served as a royal residence and fortress. Its impressive architecture and historical significance make it a must-visit landmark.

San Gregorio Armeno

San Gregorio Armeno is known as the "street of nativity scenes," famous for its artisan workshops that create nativity figurines and scenes. A stroll here is an immersion into Neapolitan tradition.

Monastery of Santa Chiara

The Monastery of Santa Chiara is a stunning religious complex famous for its Gothic church and beautiful cloister adorned with majolica tiles.

Capodimonte Museum

The Capodimonte Museum houses a prestigious collection of Renaissance and Baroque paintings, including masterpieces by Caravaggio, Titian, and Raphael.

San Martino Charterhouse

This former monastery offers stunning views over Naples and houses a museum showcasing Neapolitan art and history, including nativity scenes and historical artifacts.

Castel Sant'Elmo

Castel Sant'Elmo, a medieval fortress located on the Vomero hill, offers one of the best panoramic views of Naples. The castle, with its strategic position, played a crucial role in the city's defense and now hosts exhibitions and cultural events.
